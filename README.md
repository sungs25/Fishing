Fishing 🎣
An interactive art piece about online hatred, built as an Android app.
Touch the screen. Watch what happens.
A quiet surface. Gentle waves. Peaceful words drifting in six languages — "so cute," "귀여워," "いいね," "wholesome," "太棒了," "süß."
But you touch anyway.
The moment your finger drops, it becomes bait. Words rush toward it like fish to feed — curious at first, then aggressive, then frenzied. Across every language, the same transformation. The screen darkens. The water turns red. The words grow, shake, and swarm.
Then the bait runs out. And silence returns. As if nothing happened.
But something did change. Some words never fully recover. They return to the surface looking peaceful — but now they say "mid," "ㅋㅋ," "whatever," "meh." They've been soaked in hate long enough that cynicism became their default.
The Question
If you know it's bait, why do you keep touching?
How It Works

Touch = drop bait. Words swarm toward it like a feeding frenzy.
Release = the bait slowly runs out. Words scatter and calm returns.
Repeat = some words get permanently corrupted. Peace looks the same, but the words are different.

No server. No database. No social network. Just a screen, your finger, and the words that follow.
Tech

Kotlin, Android native
Custom View + Canvas rendering
No XML layouts, no Compose, no game engine
Touch input, real-time animation loop via postInvalidateOnAnimation()
6 languages: Korean, English, Japanese, Chinese, German, Spanish

Download the APK from itch.io and install on any Android device.
